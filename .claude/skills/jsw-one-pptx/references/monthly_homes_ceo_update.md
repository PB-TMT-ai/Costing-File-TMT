# Monthly Homes CEO Update — Deck Construction Guide

**Read this file when the user asks for a monthly Homes update, Homes business review, Homes CEO review, or Homes performance deck.**

---

## Context

- **Audience:** CEO + Homes BU head + key functional leads (5-8 people)
- **Cadence:** Monthly, presented at T+5 to T+10 after month close
- **Purpose:** End-to-end performance review of JSW One Homes: funnel health (leads to handover), city-level execution, material monetization (GMV), service revenue (GSV), design and delivery operations. This deck answers: "Are we acquiring customers, converting them, building on time, and monetizing materials?"
- **Lens:** Business performance — funnel velocity, conversion efficiency, city-level P&L-equivalent, AOP achievement, YoY growth trajectory, and forward projection.
- **Density:** Moderate-high. CEO-level but with city granularity. Tables with color-coded achievement %. Commentary is brief, action-oriented.
- **Title style:** Topic titles throughout. Insight titles only for the performance dashboard opener.
- **Typical slide count:** 12-16 slides (including title, highlights, and thank you)

## Homes Terminology — Critical Definitions

| Term | Meaning | Why it matters |
|------|---------|---------------|
| **M5** | Milestone 5 — design agreement signed (customer commits) | Pipeline creation. The "top of funnel" conversion event. |
| **M8** | Milestone 8 — construction payment milestone (customer pays for build to start) | The money event. M8 is when revenue becomes real. |
| **M9** | Milestone 9 — construction progress payment | Construction velocity indicator. |
| **GSV** | Gross Service Value — project management, design, and franchise fees (INR Cr) | Fee-based revenue. JSW One's "service" revenue stream. |
| **GMV** | Gross Merchandise Value — material sales (TMT, cement, RMC, allied) (INR Cr) | Material monetization. The cross-sell thesis. |
| **QL** | Qualified Leads — marketing-qualified inbound leads | Top of funnel. Marketing effectiveness. |
| **QL %** | QL as % of total leads | Lead quality metric. Target: ~16%. |
| **M5 Gross** | Total M5 signed (before refunds/cancellations) | Volume. |
| **M5 Net** | M5 Gross minus refunds/cancellations | True pipeline after churn. |
| **M8 Net** | M8 Gross minus refunds | True construction commitments. |
| **Refund M5** | Customers who cancelled after signing M5 (design churn) | Lost pipeline. Each M5 refund = wasted design cost + lost GMV. |
| **Refund M8** | Customers who cancelled after starting construction (rare, serious) | High-severity churn. Construction waste + customer failure. |
| **BOQ** | Bill of Quantities — detailed material list for a project | Drives GMV accuracy. BOQ TAT = speed of pricing response. |
| **GFC** | Good For Construction — final design approved for build | Design completion milestone. GFC count = projects ready to build. |
| **DBD** | Design Before Development — detailed design stage | Intermediate design milestone between Schematic and GFC. |
| **Schematic** | Initial design concept delivered to customer | First design output. |
| **R-Dash** | Real-time Dashboard — compliance tracking for operations | Process adherence metric. |
| **Construction Start TAT** | Days from M8 payment to actual construction start | Target: 60 days. If >60, something is blocking (permits, contractor, material). |
| **On-time delivery %** | % of handovers completed within original committed timeline | Execution quality. Target: 90%. |
| **NPS** | Net Promoter Score | Customer satisfaction. Track average score and promoter count. |
| **L4 Escalation** | Level 4 escalation — serious customer complaint | Quality failure. Track raised + closure rate. |
| **Under Construction #** | Active projects currently in build phase | WIP inventory. Should grow steadily. |
| **Handovers** | Completed projects delivered to customer | Ultimate output metric. |
| **Allied + Non-Homes GMV** | Material sold for non-standard categories (paints, electrical, plumbing) or outside Homes projects | Incremental GMV. Track separately from core Homes GMV. |

## The Homes Funnel — The Core Analytical Framework

Every Homes deck should be understood through the funnel:

```
LEADS (QL)
   ↓  QL% (~16% target)
QUALIFIED LEADS
   ↓  M5 Conversion % (~3-6% of QL → M5)
M5 SIGNED (Design agreement)
   ↓  M5→M8 Conversion % (~50-70%)
M8 SIGNED (Construction payment)
   ↓  Construction Start TAT (target: 60 days)
UNDER CONSTRUCTION
   ↓  On-time delivery % (target: 90%)
HANDOVER (completed)
   ↓
GSV realized + GMV realized
```

**Each stage has a leakage point.** The analytical pre-computation must identify WHERE in the funnel the loss is happening:
- Low QL → marketing spend or channel issue
- Low M5 conversion → design quality or pricing issue
- Low M8 conversion → customer financing or sticker shock
- Low construction starts → contractor availability or material supply
- Low handovers → execution delays or quality rework
- Low GMV/GSV → cross-sell failure or pricing

## Analytical Pre-Steps — Run Before Building

1. **Funnel conversion rates:** Compute QL→M5 conversion %, M5→M8 conversion %, M8→Handover conversion % for current month and YTD. Compare vs FY25 same period. Identify which stage has the biggest drop.

2. **City ranking by AOP gap:** Rank cities by absolute GSV gap (GSV Target - GSV Achieved). The top 2-3 cities explain most of the total miss. Lead the commentary with these cities.

3. **City ranking by YoY growth:** Flag any city with negative YoY M5 growth — this is a demand contraction signal. Flag any city where M5 conversion % declined YoY — this is a funnel efficiency loss.

4. **Refund rate:** Compute M5 refund rate = Refund M5 / M5 Gross (YTD). M8 refund rate = Refund M8 / M8 Gross. If M5 refund rate >10%, design or pricing is causing drop-offs. If M8 refund rate >5%, construction anxiety or financing failure.

5. **GSV per handover and GMV per handover:** Compute: GSV YTD / Handovers YTD, GMV YTD / Handovers YTD. These are unit economics. If GSV/handover is declining, either project sizes are shrinking or fee realization is weakening.

6. **GMV mix — material cross-sell health:** Compute: TMT % of GMV, Cement/RMC % of GMV, Allied + Non-Homes % of GMV. If Allied is growing disproportionately, the "core Homes GMV" might be stagnant. Separate core from non-core.

7. **Design TAT breach:** If Design TAT (target: 45/60 days) is >70 days, the design team is the bottleneck slowing M5→M8 conversion. Flag with specific number.

8. **Construction Start TAT:** If >60 days, flag. Identify whether delay is permit, contractor, or material (commentary from BU).

9. **On-time delivery %:** If <80%, flag. This directly impacts NPS and customer referrals. Quantify: how many projects are delayed and by how much?

10. **Hockey-stick in projection:** Check Feb-Mar estimates vs Oct-Jan actuals. If Feb+Mar GMV is >40% of full-year GMV, flag as concentration risk.

11. **Studio maturation:** For any city operational <9 months, track M5 per month trend separately. Don't compare new studios to mature ones on achievement %.

## Key Analytical Patterns for Homes

### Funnel velocity is the north star

Every Homes deck is fundamentally about the funnel. The CEO's first question is: "Are we converting leads into paying customers?" The second is: "Are we delivering on time?"

If M5 is on track but M8 is lagging → design-to-construction conversion is the problem (customer drop-off after seeing final price, or financing).

If M8 is on track but handovers are lagging → execution is the problem (contractor delays, material supply, permits).

If both M5 and M8 are lagging → demand is the problem (marketing, lead quality, city-level market conditions).

### GSV and GMV are two separate stories

GSV = fee revenue. It grows with project count and project value.
GMV = material revenue. It grows with material consumption per project AND cross-sell penetration.

**A common failure mode:** GSV grows (more projects signed) but GMV lags (material not sold through JSW One, customer sources independently). This means the cross-sell thesis is failing. Track GMV/GSV ratio monthly — it should be stable or improving.

### City-level variance drives the total

Homes is a city-by-city business. Total numbers are meaningless without city breakdown. A city like Bangalore doing INR 20 Cr GSV masks that it's at 73% achievement. A city like Bhubaneswar at 55% achievement is the drag.

**Always rank cities by absolute gap, not just %.** Bangalore at 73% on a INR 27 Cr target (gap: INR 7 Cr) matters more than Bellary at 107% on a INR 1.7 Cr target.

### Refunds are hidden churn

M5 refunds = customer cancelled design contract. M8 refunds = customer cancelled construction contract.

M5 refunds are common (3-5% is normal). M8 refunds are serious (should be <2%). If M8 refunds spike, investigate: is it financing failure, sticker shock from final BOQ, or competitor poaching?
