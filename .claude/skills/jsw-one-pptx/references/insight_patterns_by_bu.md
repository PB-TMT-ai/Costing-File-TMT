# Insight Patterns by Business Unit & Enhanced Deck Blueprints

**Read this file when building any deck that covers specific JSW One business units. It provides BU-specific "so-what" patterns — concrete templates for what a sharp insight looks like given real data conditions in each BU. Also contains analytical pre-steps for each deck type blueprint.**

---

## 1. How to Use This File

Each BU section follows the same structure:

1. **Key metrics** — what to compute and surface
2. **Signal patterns** — "if you see X, the insight is about Y"
3. **Common narratives** — recurring stories in this BU
4. **Red flags** — thresholds that trigger alerts
5. **Positive signals** — what "good" looks like

**Rule: The insight is never the metric itself.** "Volume was 525 KMT" is not an insight. "Volume missed by 30%, concentrated in 3 states where dealer activation stalled" is an insight.

## 2. Manufacturing — Enterprise (T1S / Plant Supply)

### Key metrics
Volume (KMT), GMV, Revenue, GM/MT, customer count, average order size (MT/order), repeat rate, product mix (HR/CR/HRPO/Coated/WR/GP/PPGL shares), top-10 customer concentration (% of volume), non-JSW supply share (%)

### Signal patterns

| If you see... | The insight is about... | Slide framing |
|--------------|------------------------|---------------|
| Volume up but GM/MT flat or down | Price competition or adverse product mix | "Volume growth is dilutive — GM/MT declined INR X despite Y% growth, suggesting adverse mix toward lower-margin HR" |
| Top-10 concentration >60% | Customer risk | "Top 10 drive X% of Enterprise volume — any single loss creates INR Y Cr quarterly gap" |
| Average order size increasing | Wallet share deepening | "Avg order grew from X to Y MT (+Z%), indicating deepening wallet share" |
| CR/Coated share increasing | Product premiumization | "High-margin CR/Coated now X% of mix, up from Y% — each point of mix shift adds ~INR Z/MT" |
| Non-JSW share growing | Supply diversification (positive) | "Non-JSW sources now X% of Enterprise, up from Y% — reduces parent dependency" |

### Common narratives
- Enterprise is the scale engine — tonnage that covers fixed costs
- JSW Steel plant supply is captive demand; true growth comes from non-JSW sources
- Structurally low-margin (1-2% GM) but high-volume — contribution is to absorb opex

### Red flags
- Customer concentration >70%
- GM/MT below INR 500 for 2+ months
- Volume decline >10% QoQ without seasonal explanation

### Positive signals
- Non-JSW share crossing 25% (strategic diversification milestone)
- Consecutive quarter AOP beats (most reliable BU in portfolio)
- Product mix shifting toward value-added (CR/Coated >30%)

## 3. Manufacturing — SMB (Distributed)

### Key metrics
Volume (KMT), GMV, Revenue, GM/MT, CM1/MT, JODL vs marketplace split (1P vs 3P %), distributor count, active distributors, per-distributor throughput, credit penetration %, geographic spread (states/districts)

### Signal patterns

| If you see... | The insight is about... | Slide framing |
|--------------|------------------------|---------------|
| JODL share increasing | Margin accretion but capital intensity | "JODL grew from X% to Y% — adds INR Z/MT to margin but increases WC by ~INR A Cr/quarter" |
| JODL share >35% | Capital efficiency risk | "JODL at X% — beyond 30%, each point requires disproportionate capital, threatening the capital efficiency ratio" |
| Credit penetration up but GM/MT flat | Credit is a volume lever, not margin | "Credit attachment up from X% to Y%, but GM/MT unchanged — credit enables volume, not margin" |
| Per-distributor throughput declining | Over-distribution | "Added X distributors but throughput fell to Y MT — network outpacing demand generation" |
| Multi-brand share increasing | Reduced JSW dependency (positive) | "Non-JSW brands now X% — reduces single-source dependency, improves negotiating position" |

### Common narratives
- The hybrid model in action: JODL (25-30%) for margin, marketplace (70-75%) for scale
- 100% JODL destroys capital efficiency (2-3x more capital needed); 100% marketplace at ~1% take rates can't cover costs
- Tier 2/3 geographic expansion is the runway, but 6-9 month breakeven per new geography

### Red flags
- JODL share >35%
- Per-distributor throughput declining 3+ months
- Credit default rate >3%

### Positive signals
- New geography achieving breakeven in <6 months
- JODL share stable at 25-30% while absolute JODL margin/MT improving

## 4. Construction — Projects & Retail

### Key metrics
Volume (KMT), GMV, Revenue, GM/MT, CM1/MT, product mix (TMT/Cement/RMC shares), customer segmentation, OTIF %, geographic concentration, credit penetration %

### Signal patterns

| If you see... | The insight is about... | Slide framing |
|--------------|------------------------|---------------|
| TMT growing faster than cement/RMC | Single-category dependency | "TMT is X% of Construction — cement/RMC cross-sell at Y%, below plan" |
| Projects seasonal (Q3/Q4 heavy) | Infrastructure spending cycle | "Q3-Q4 = X% of Projects volume — H2 pipeline covers Y% of target" |
| Retail customer count up, repeat low | Acquisition without retention | "Added X customers but D30 at Y% — each churned customer costs ~INR Z in CAC" |
| OTIF below 85% | Service quality impact | "OTIF at X% — Y% of delays supply-side vs Z% last-mile" |
| Volume spike in one state | Large project win — check durability | "State X doubled QoQ — verify: one-off project or recurring demand" |
| Cement volume exceeding TMT growth | Cross-sell working | "Cement grew X% vs TMT Y% — cross-sell thesis validating; cement carries higher margin/MT" |

### Common narratives
- Construction Retail is the bridge to Private Brands — marketplace dealers may convert to PB
- Projects volume is lumpy and relationship-driven; don't overgeneralize one quarter
- Cement and RMC cross-sell alongside steel is the margin uplift play

### Red flags
- OTIF below 80% for 2+ consecutive months
- AOP stretch factor >2x (this BU has historically the weakest AOP achievement)
- Single project >20% of quarterly volume

### Positive signals
- Cross-sell rate (non-steel % of Construction GMV) exceeding 15%
- OTIF above 90% sustained
- Credit penetration crossing 25% (enables stickier relationships)

## 5. Private Brands (JSW One TMT, One Helix TMT, One Helix P&T)

### Key metrics
Volume (KMT), GMV, Revenue, GM/MT, CM1/MT, Retail vs Projects split, dealer count (total/active/star), D30/D60/D90 repeat rates (counter-level), state-wise volume, distributor count, DCI/DPI scores, distributor NPS, brand-wise split (JSW One TMT Fe550D/Fe550 vs One Helix Fe550), CM utilization, OTIF

### Signal patterns

| If you see... | The insight is about... | Slide framing |
|--------------|------------------------|---------------|
| Volume up but active dealer ratio declining | Top-heavy growth, bottom dormant | "Volume grew X% but active ratio fell to Y% — bottom 50% of dealers dormant" |
| D30 repeat below 40% | Dealer economics unsustainable | "D30 at X% = 6 of 10 dealers don't reorder in 30 days — unit economics negative until D30 crosses 50%" |
| CM1/MT gap: Retail vs Projects | Channel economics divergence | "Retail CM1 (INR X) nearly 2x Projects (INR Y) — each point shift to Retail adds INR Z Cr" |
| One Helix accelerating | Second brand gaining traction | "One Helix now X% of PB volume — validates dual-brand strategy for secondary markets" |
| CM1/MT dip Jul-Sep | Seasonal + pricing policy impact | "CM1/MT drops from ~INR 700 to ~INR 400 in monsoon — structural: lower demand + pricing policy protection costs. This is NOT a performance failure; it repeats annually" |
| Star dealer growth lagging total | Dealer quality diluting | "Star dealers grew X vs Y total new — new dealers are not high-volume; revise onboarding filters" |
| State concentration >40% | Geographic risk | "State X = Y% of PB volume — regulatory/CM/distributor disruption has outsized impact" |
| Pricing policy losses >2% of PB revenue | Margin governance issue | "Pricing policy absorbed INR X Cr in losses — double-loss problem: JSW One bears downside in both price-up and price-down scenarios" |
| Projects volume outpacing Retail | Channel mix risk to margin | "Projects share grew to X% — lower CM1/MT channel pulling down blended margin; prioritize Retail dealer activation" |
| Negative CM/MT on specific plant-state routes | Route-level margin leakage | "X negative-margin routes lost INR Y Lac in [Month] — recommend: reprice or reroute to nearest profitable CM" |
| DSI >25 days at a CM plant | Working capital locked in inventory | "Plant X at Y days DSI, INR Z Cr locked — sales plan needed to liquidate; if DSI drops to 15 days, releases INR A Cr" |
| Advances >90 days to a CM | Governance escalation | "INR X Cr stuck with CM Y for >90 days — escalation required; interest cost drag of INR Z Lac/month" |
| Salesperson Qty/Pax <200 MT in a state | Sales inefficiency | "State X at Y MT/pax vs platform avg Z MT/pax — employee cost/MT exceeds CM1/MT; not self-funding" |
| Stocking ratio >30% in a state | Distributor hoarding | "State X stocking ratio at Y% — volume inflated by distributor self-stocking, not sell-through. Verify secondary sales" |
| MOU achievement <50% for major distributors | Forward volume risk | "X distributors at <50% MOU achievement — Y KMT volume at risk (Z% of remaining annual plan)" |
| BE CM1/MT vs actual CM1/MT divergence >20% | Pricing or cost surprise | "Actual CM1/MT INR X vs BE of INR Y — Z% gap driven by [purchase cost surprise / freight / volume mix]" |

### Common narratives
- PB is the most watched BU — the only fully owned brand with structural margin (3-4% GM vs 1-2% marketplace)
- AOP targets are consistently the most aggressive in the portfolio (track actual stretch factor from latest data, do not hardcode)
- FY26 PB achieved only 43% of its AOP — worst of any BU. Historical achievement rate must anchor any forward-looking PB slide
- Pricing policy creates "double loss" — JSW One absorbs downside in both price-up and price-down scenarios; material leakage risk on thin margins
- Dealer activation is the LEADING indicator. Volume is LAGGING. Track dealer activation weekly
- One Helix (non-JSW brand) is for markets where JSW brand is weaker or price competition is fiercer
- **One Helix P&T (Pipes & Tubes):** Newest product vertical. Structural retail tubes market ~4 Mn MT, growing 12% CAGR. MS tubes 60%+ of market. P&T is a separate entity (One Helix brand), separate distribution from TMT. When P&T data appears, treat it as a separate sub-BU within PB slides — do NOT combine P&T volumes with TMT volumes without labeling

### Red flags
- AOP stretch factor >2.5x (compute fresh each time, do not rely on cached numbers)
- Active dealer ratio below 50%
- D30 below 35%
- Any single CM >30% of PB supply (supply concentration)
- CM1/MT below INR 400 for 2+ months outside monsoon
- Pricing policy losses exceeding 2% of PB revenue
- DSI weighted avg >25 days (working capital drag)
- Advance to CM >90 days outstanding (governance escalation)
- Total INR loss on negative-margin routes exceeding 1% of PB revenue
- Salesperson Qty/Pax below 200 MT/month in any state for 2+ months
- Stocking ratio >30% for any state (distributor hoarding risk)
- MOU achievement <50% for distributors responsible for >20% of plan volume

### Positive signals
- D30 crossing 50% (self-sustaining dealer base milestone)
- Active dealer ratio above 65%
- One Helix reaching 20%+ of PB volume (dual-brand validated)
- Retail channel exceeding 60% of PB volume (margin-accretive mix)
- New state achieving 5 KMT/month within 6 months of launch
- Projects CM/MT turning positive after consecutive negative months (inflection)
- DSI weighted avg declining below 15 days (WC efficiency milestone)
- All advances <30 days outstanding (clean WC position)
- Retail CM1/MT exceeding INR 2,500 (strong margin month)

## 6. Homes (JSW One Homes)

### Key metrics
M5 Gross/Net (monthly), M8 Gross/Net, M9, Handovers, Under Construction # (running portfolio), GSV (INR Cr — total project service value), GMV (INR Cr — material sales, split: TMT / Cement-RMC / Allied+Non-Homes), QL count and QL% (qualified lead conversion from total leads, target 16%), M5 conversion % (M5/QL), M8 conversion % (M8/M5), M9 conversion % (M9/M8), M5 refund count, M8 refund count, On-time delivery %, Construction start TAT (target 60 days), Design TAT — Schematic (target 45 days) and DBD (target 60 days), GFC TAT (target 26 days), BOQ TAT (target 2 days), R-Dash compliance %, NPS (Sales + Solutions, separately scored, target 4.0+), cost-per-M5 by city (marketing spend ÷ M5), contractor TV and Net Payment (INR Cr), studio count (live/planned)

**City portfolio (as of FY26):** Bangalore, Bellary, Bhubaneswar, Chennai, Coimbatore, Hubli, Hyderabad, Indore, Kochi, Mysuru, Nagpur, Pune, Raipur, Salem, Tiruchirappalli, Visakhapatnam

**GMV sub-categories:** TMT (core), Cement & RMC (core), Allied categories + Non-Homes (adjacent). Track core vs adjacent split — Non-Homes GMV inflates headline.

### Signal patterns

| If you see... | The insight is about... | Slide framing |
|--------------|------------------------|---------------|
| M5 below target but QLs on track | Sales conversion issue, not demand | "QL flow at X (target Y) but M5 conversion dropped to Z% — sales team converting fewer leads to committed customers" |
| M8 below target but M5 on track | Design-to-construction commitment gap | "M5 at X% of AOP but M8 at only Y% — Z customers signed design but haven't committed to construction; follow up on pending M8s" |
| Handovers below target but under-construction growing | Delivery execution lag | "Under construction grew to X but handovers at Y/month — portfolio turnover Z%, below 4% threshold; delivery speed is the constraint" |
| GSV below target but handovers on track | Revenue recognition lag or project value shrinkage | "X homes delivered but GSV ach at Y% — implied GSV/home declining; verify: smaller projects or % completion recognition lag?" |
| GMV lagging GSV | Material cross-sell failing | "GSV at X% ach but GMV at only Y% — material dispatch not keeping pace with construction progress; TMT/Cement ordering delayed" |
| M5 conversion below 2% in a city | Lead quality or market-fit issue | "City X M5 conversion at Y% vs platform avg Z% — either lead quality is poor or the product-market fit needs re-evaluation" |
| One city's M5 declining YoY | Demand contraction | "City X M5 down Y% YoY — consuming pipeline backlog without refilling; marketing intervention needed" |
| Refund rate >5% | Customer satisfaction red flag | "M8 refund rate at X% — each M8 refund = ~INR 20-40 Lac lost GSV; root cause: [cost overrun / execution delay / design mismatch]" |
| Construction start TAT >75 days | Pre-construction bottleneck | "TAT at X days vs 60-day target — Y projects stuck in pre-construction; diagnose: soil test / approvals / contractor allocation?" |
| Feb+Mar carrying >30% of FY M5 or GSV target | Hockey-stick risk | "X% of annual target loaded in final 2 months — at current monthly run rate, achievable only if monthly output doubles" |
| Under construction count declining | Pipeline drying up | "Active portfolio dropped from X to Y — new starts not replacing handovers; M5/M8 pipeline needs acceleration" |
| Allied + Non-Homes GMV growing | New revenue stream gaining traction | "Non-Homes GMV at INR X Cr (Y% of total) — adjacent revenue validates platform extension beyond core homebuilding" |
| Cost-per-M5 exceeding INR 50K in a city | Marketing ROI question | "City X: INR Y per M5 vs platform avg INR Z — verify: new market ramp-up or structural inefficiency? Exceeding INR 1 Lac = escalation" |
| GFC TAT >40 days | Construction can't start even after design | "GFC TAT at X days vs 26-day target — downstream cascade: each 10-day GFC delay pushes construction start by ~15 days" |
| R-Dash approval compliance <70% | Operational discipline gap | "R-Dash approval at X% — soil test / digital survey / SE recce approvals lagging; construction quality risk" |

### Common narratives
- Franchise model: JSW One provides design, materials, project management; execution via local contractors
- Multi-layered revenue: GSV (PM fee + design + franchise) and GMV (materials — TMT, cement, RMC, allied)
- The funnel: Leads → QLs (16%) → M5 (design sign-off) → M8 (construction commitment) → Under Construction → Handover
- Strategy is depth over breadth — deepen existing cities (Bangalore, Chennai, Coimbatore, Hyderabad, Salem) before new launches
- Homes is the ultimate cross-sell vehicle: every project consumes TMT, cement, paints, pipes, fittings. GMV/GSV ratio measures this
- City-level variance is extreme — top cities can be at 100%+ AOP while bottom cities are at 30%. Platform averages mask this. Always show city-level data

### Red flags
- Handovers declining QoQ for 2+ quarters
- On-time delivery % below 80% (systemic execution issue)
- Construction start TAT exceeding 75 days
- M5 conversion % below 2% platform-wide
- M8 refund rate above 5%
- Under construction count declining for 2+ months (pipeline drying)
- Any city with M5 achievement <40% of FY AOP at YTD point
- GSV achievement <60% of YTD target (revenue risk)
- GMV achievement <40% of YTD target (material cross-sell failure)
- Design TAT (Schematic) exceeding 85 days (vs 45-60 target) — cascading delivery delay
- Cost-per-M5 exceeding INR 1 Lac in any city (immediate escalation)
- GFC TAT exceeding 40 days (vs 26-day target — blocking construction start)
- R-Dash approval compliance below 70% (operational discipline gap)

**City maturity classification (use for commentary weighting):**
- Scaling (>5 M5/month sustained): Bangalore, Coimbatore, Indore — misses here matter most
- Growing (2-5 M5/month): Chennai, Hubli, Nagpur, Pune, Bhubaneswar, Hyderabad
- Early (<2 M5/month): Kochi, Mysuru, Raipur, Salem, new launches
- At risk (declining M5 for 3+ months or <1/month): any city falling here — flag for studio review

### Positive signals
- M5 conversion % above 4% (strong marketing-to-sales efficiency)
- On-time delivery above 90% sustained
- Under construction count growing steadily (scaling signal)
- GMV/GSV ratio improving (material monetization deepening)
- Handover count exceeding 15/month (run rate for 200+ FY target)
- City achieving >100% AOP on both GSV and GMV (execution excellence)
- Allied + Non-Homes GMV contributing >10% of total GMV (new revenue validated)
- NPS average above 9.0 (promoter category)
- Portfolio turnover >5%/month (healthy delivery pace)
- Cost-per-M5 below INR 25K in a mature city (efficient marketing)
- New city reaching 3+ M5/month within 6 months of launch (fast ramp)

## 7. Credit (3P Orchestration + JOFL)

### Key metrics
Credit-enabled GMV, credit penetration %, 3P vs JOFL split, JOFL book size, disbursement volume, avg ticket size, NPA/provision rate, DPD >30/90, approval rate, time-to-disburse

### Signal patterns

| If you see... | The insight is about... | Slide framing |
|--------------|------------------------|---------------|
| Penetration rising toward 40% | Strategic target approaching | "Credit penetration at X% vs 40-50% target — each point adds ~INR Y Cr to credit-enabled GMV" |
| JOFL growing faster than 3P | Balance sheet risk shifting to 1P | "JOFL share grew to X% — every INR 100 Cr on JOFL book requires proportionate capital" |
| NPA >2% | Underwriting quality issue | "JOFL NPA at X% — above 2% watchpoint; tighten on highest-default segment" |
| Approval rate falling | Tightening vs quality decline | "Approval rate X% to Y% — confirm: intentional risk tightening or application quality declining?" |
| 3P lender count growing | Platform credibility expanding | "X new lending partners onboarded — reduces JOFL dependency, validates platform credit model" |

### Common narratives
- Credit is an enabler, not a standalone profit center. Value = incremental GMV enabled
- 3P orchestration (banks/NBFCs) is preferred — keeps risk off balance sheet
- JOFL serves as fallback + data asset for underwriting improvement
- Target: 40-50% of GMV on credit

### Red flags
- JOFL DPD >30 exceeding 5%
- JOFL book growth >50% QoQ without proportionate capital/provisions
- 3P lender attrition

### Positive signals
- Credit penetration crossing 35%
- 3P lender count exceeding 5
- JOFL NPA below 1.5% sustained

## 8. Platform-Level Patterns (Cross-BU)

### Signal patterns

| If you see... | The insight is about... | Slide framing |
|--------------|------------------------|---------------|
| GMV up but revenue slower | Take-rate compression or BU mix shift | "GMV +X% but revenue +Y% — take-rate declined from A% to B%" |
| Volume up but EBITDA margin flat | No operating leverage | "Volume +X% but EBITDA margin unchanged — opex grew proportionally; leverage not visible" |
| Opex/revenue declining | Operating leverage kicking in | "Opex/revenue improved X% to Y% — each INR 100 Cr incremental revenue drops ~INR Z Cr to EBITDA" |
| NWC days increasing | Growth consuming cash | "NWC crept from X to Y — INR Z Cr additional WC deployed; temporary or structural?" |
| Capital efficiency declining | Investor narrative risk | "Capital efficiency X.Xx, down from 11.9x — THE investor metric; below 8x, peer differentiation narrows" |
| Customer count up but GMV/customer flat | Breadth without depth | "Added X customers but GMV/customer at INR Y Cr, unchanged — new customers not scaling" |
| Repeat rate improving across BUs | Platform stickiness building | "Platform repeat rate crossed X% — strongest evidence of ecosystem lock-in for DRHP narrative" |
| JODL revenue growing faster than marketplace | 1P tilting model | "JODL now X% of consolidated revenue — deliberate if for margin; risky if capital efficiency erodes" |

### Positive inflection signals (surface these when they appear)
- EBITDA breakeven month or quarter (milestone for any pre-profit company)
- Platform volume crossing 1 MTPA or key GMV thresholds (INR 20,000 Cr etc.)
- Customer count milestones (5,000, 10,000 etc.)
- Multiple BUs beating AOP simultaneously (execution quality signal)
- NWC days improving while volume grows (capital discipline at scale)

## 9. Enhanced Deck Blueprint Pre-Steps

### 9.1 Before building a Board Deck (Quarterly)

1. Compute QoQ and YoY variance for all BUs (volume, GMV, revenue, GM, EBITDA)
2. Rank BUs by absolute contribution to total EBITDA variance vs AOP
3. Identify #1 positive surprise and #1 negative surprise
4. Compute working capital (NWC days, receivable days, cash balance) vs prior quarter and vs peers
5. Check for inflection points (breakeven, milestone crossing)
6. Run web search for material peer developments (competition decks only)

**Exec summary framed by #2 and #3. Working capital slide framed by #4.**

### 9.2 Before building a Monthly S&OP Review

1. Compute actuals vs monthly AOP target (not annual pro-rata)
2. Calculate YTD achievement % by BU
3. Identify any BU where YTD achievement <80% — these drive remediation discussion
4. Compute OTIF and delivery metrics trend
5. Check PB dealer activation and repeat rate
6. Review prior month's action items — completed vs slipped

**Opening snapshot framed by #2 and #3.**

### 9.3 Before building a Monthly Financial & Profitability Report

1. Compute all 17-column monthly trend data (Apr to current + MoM% + YTD ach%)
2. Run P&L variance: revenue, GM, EBITDA vs AOP — isolate 2-3 biggest EBITDA drivers
3. Entity-wise profitability: JOPL standalone, JODL standalone, consolidated — verify reconciliation
4. T1S state-wise margin ranking (if data available)
5. JODL per-MT economics trend: revenue/MT, cost/MT, margin/MT
6. Flag any line item with MoM movement >20%
7. Compute employee cost/head, marketing/revenue — check for runaway cost lines
8. Compute operating leverage ratios (Section 2.8 of analytical_engine.md)

**Key insights slide framed by #2. EBITDA bridge from #2. Entity splits from #3.**

### 9.4 Before building a Competition Analysis Deck

1. Run web search per competitor (news, funding, leadership, IPO)
2. Pull latest financials from project knowledge or uploaded files
3. Compute YoY revenue growth, margin trend (3-year), ROCE for each competitor
4. Compare against JSW One: revenue growth, capital efficiency, NWC, GM%, EBITDA margin
5. Identify governance flags: auditor qualifications, MSME delays, RPT exposure, credit ratings
6. Rank competitors by: (a) revenue proximity, (b) margin improvement, (c) governance risk

**Deck ordered by threat level, not alphabetically.**

### 9.5 Before building an AOP Deck

1. Compute stretch factor per BU: target / latest actual (NOT prior AOP)
2. Compare vs historical achievement rates (last 4 quarters)
3. Classify: Achievable (<1.3x, >75% hit), Stretch (1.3-2x), High (>2x, <50% hit), Critical (>3x)
4. EBITDA sensitivity: per BU, what's the EBITDA impact of a 10% volume miss?
5. Identify top 3 assumptions the AOP rests on
6. Compute capital required: working capital for incremental volume + capex
7. Run hockey-stick detection (Section 2.9 of analytical_engine.md) on quarterly phasing

**Risk slide (mandatory) framed by #2, #3, #4. Capital discussion from #6. Seasonality caveat from #7.**

## 10. Tone and Language Rules

### Analyst vs consultant — the difference

| Consultant says | Analyst says |
|----------------|-------------|
| "Significant opportunity to improve..." | "PB needs 40% H2 acceleration — current run rate misses by 25%" |
| "Recommend focusing on growth drivers..." | "Three assumptions underpin the turnaround: PB, Construction, JODL mix. Two assessed as Unlikely" |
| "Demonstrated strong momentum..." | "Volume CAGR 115% (FY22-26), but decelerating from 184% to 48% as base effect kicks in" |
| "Working capital remains a priority..." | "NWC at 12 days. Drift to 20 days at FY27 volumes = INR 180 Cr additional capital locked" |
| "Customer retention has improved..." | "D30: 32% to 44%. Still below 50% threshold. Improvement stalled in Q3" |

### Core principle

Quantify everything. Replace adjectives with numbers. Replace "significant" with the magnitude. Replace "improvement" with the bps or % change. Replace "risk" with the INR Cr downside if it materializes.

### Banned phrases (never use on any slide)

- "Robust growth" / "strong momentum" / "healthy trajectory" — say the actual growth rate
- "Significant" / "substantial" / "notable" / "impressive" / "considerable" — say the number
- "Holistic approach" / "synergies" / "paradigm shift" / "ecosystem play" — say what specifically
- "Leveraging" (as a verb meaning "using") — say "using" or "scaling" or name the action
- "Going forward" / "In the near term" — say the quarter or month
- "Stakeholder alignment" / "cross-functional collaboration" — name who and what they need to do
- "Optimize" (when vague) — say what metric improves by how much
- "Key takeaway" (as a section title on every slide) — the title IS the takeaway; a separate box is redundant

These words are fillers that mask the absence of an actual insight. If removing the adjective makes the sentence empty, the sentence has no insight.
