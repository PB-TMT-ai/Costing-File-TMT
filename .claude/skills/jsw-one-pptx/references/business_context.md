## 12. JSW One — Business Context Reference

**Use this section to understand JSW One's business when creating decks outside the main project. This ensures slides use correct terminology, segment names, and business logic.**

### 11.1 Company Overview

JSW One Platforms Limited (JOPL) is a pre-IPO, full-stack B2B digital marketplace for construction and manufacturing MSMEs. Launched in April 2021 as part of the JSW Group, it integrates procurement, logistics, financing, and private brands into a single digital ecosystem.

**Entity structure:**
- **JOPL** (JSW One Platforms Limited) — the parent holding company and marketplace platform
- **JODL** (JSW One Distribution Limited) — wholly-owned subsidiary, 1P stock-and-sell distribution arm. JOTS (logistics) is stationed within JODL.
- **JOFL** (JSW One Finance Limited) — wholly-owned subsidiary, captive NBFC for lending
- **One Helix** — wholly-owned subsidiary, new entity to launch non-JSW-branded products. First category: Pipes & Tubes under the One Helix brand.
- **JOTS** (JSW One Transportation Services) — logistics vertical within JODL, serving all BUs

**Key facts:**
- HQ: Mumbai; operations across 20+ states
- Team: ~800 professionals; in-house tech team of 125+
- Business model: Full-stack B2B marketplace integrating commerce, distribution, logistics, financing, and private brands
- Customer base: 6,200+ transacting customers; ~70% repeat rate
- IPO status: DRHP in preparation
- Key investors: Mitsui & Co. (Series A)
- **For actual GMV, volume, EBITDA, and growth projections — always refer to the latest AOP, financial model, or board-approved data files provided by the user. Do NOT embed specific forward-looking numbers in slides without a user-provided source file.**

### 11.2 Business Units — Detailed Reference

**When creating slides, use this to determine which BU context applies. If a user says "make a slide on manufacturing", it means the Commerce stack (Enterprise + SMB). If they say "private brands", it means the PB stack specifically. If they say "JSW One overall", it means the consolidated platform view.**

#### Manufacturing — Enterprise (T1S / Plant Supply)
- **What:** Direct steel supply from JSW Steel plants to large manufacturers procuring >2,000 MT/month
- **Entity:** Revenue flows through JOPL marketplace; supply from JSW Steel
- **Products:** HR, CR, HRPO, Coated (GP, PPGL), Wire Rods
- **Customer type:** Large manufacturers, profilers, fabricators, aggregators
- **Revenue model:** Commission on material sales, platform usage charges
- **Key metrics:** Volume (MT), unique customers transacting, demand vs allocation, new customer additions
- **Slide context:** Show supply-demand matching, customer activation funnel, product-wise volume breakdowns

#### Manufacturing — SMB (Distributed)
- **What:** Multi-brand steel distribution to small/medium manufacturers procuring up to 2,000 MT/month
- **Entity:** Sold primarily via JODL (1P) as master distributor; also 3P sellers on marketplace
- **Products:** HR, CR, HRPO, GP, PPGL, WR, plus upcoming stainless steel, alloy steel, copper
- **Customer type:** Small and medium enterprises across industry segments
- **Revenue model:** Trading margin (buy-sell via JODL), platform commission (3P), VAS revenue (CTL, slitting, bending)
- **Key metrics:** Volume (MT), JODL vs 3P mix, seller count, state-wise performance, VAS penetration
- **Slide context:** Show JODL expansion by state, product category diversification, VAS adoption rates

#### Construction — Projects
- **What:** Steel + cement supply to large and multi-site construction contractors
- **Entity:** JOPL marketplace + JODL distribution
- **Products:** TMT rebar, cement, RMC
- **Customer type:** Large contractors, institutional buyers (Zetwerk, OfBusiness, Hella Infra historically)
- **Revenue model:** Commission, trading margin on TMT/cement
- **Key metrics:** Volume (MT), project pipeline, institutional vs direct split
- **Slide context:** Show project pipeline, geographic expansion, pricing competitiveness

#### Construction — Retail
- **What:** TMT, cement, RMC, paints, AAC blocks to small contractors and retailers
- **Entity:** JOPL marketplace; cross-sell with Private Brands
- **Products:** TMT (JSW + brands), cement (multiple), RMC, paints (upcoming), AAC blocks (upcoming)
- **Customer type:** Small contractors, retailers, individual builders
- **Revenue model:** Commission, cross-sell revenue
- **Key metrics:** Volume (MT), retailer count (target: 3,000+), cross-sell rate, repeat %
- **Slide context:** Show retailer footprint expansion, category penetration, cross-sell between cement/TMT

#### Private Brands (JSW One TMT, One Helix TMT, One Helix P&T)
- **What:** Asset-light branded construction materials — BIS-certified TMT rebar manufactured by 6 contract manufacturers under JSW QA/QC protocols. Sold via dedicated distributor-dealer-retailer network.
- **Entity:** JSW One TMT and One Helix TMT under JOPL; One Helix P&T under One Helix entity (no JSW logo)
- **Products:** JSW One TMT (Fe550 grade), One Helix TMT, One Helix Pipes & Tubes
- **Distribution:** 50 distributors, 4,000+ dealers, 2,000+ retailers across 20 states, 300+ districts (North, East, Central India)
- **Revenue model:** Manufacturing margin (spread between raw material cost and retail price), distribution margin via JODL
- **GTM strategy:** Influencer-orchestrated commerce (3,000+ influencer engagements in 200+ cities), dedicated sales + QC + supply teams, expansion via distributors to build retail presence, nodal agency approvals (MP Housing Board, MP PWD, UP Metro)
- **Key metrics:** Volume (KMT), D30/D60/D90 repeat rate (count + volume), dealer activation rate, average purchase frequency, churn rate, distributor count, state-wise volume, supply source mix
- **Slide context:** Show geographic expansion maps, repeat rate dashboards, distributor performance, supply-demand by state, influencer ROI, QC compliance
- **Pricing:** Retail price list mechanism; pricing policy creates asymmetric risk — JSW One bears downside in both price increases and decreases (the "double loss problem")

#### Homes (JSW One Homes)
- **What:** End-to-end homebuilding solution: design selection → material supply → contractor management → project execution, delivered via franchise studio model
- **Entity:** Operated under JOPL
- **Revenue streams:** Project management fee, franchisee and lead generation fee from franchisees, commission on material sales
- **Studio footprint:** 8 live studios (Coimbatore, Bangalore South & North, Chennai, Hyderabad East, Salem, Indore, Bellary, Hubli, Kochi); 5+ planned (Mysore, Bhubaneswar, Raipur, Hyderabad West, Pune, Vizag, Nagpur)
- **Scale:** Refer to latest corporate presentation or AOP for project pipeline numbers (design phase, construction phase counts)
- **Key metrics:** Units delivered, GTV, studio count, franchise count, design-to-construction conversion rate, project timeline adherence (target 90%+ on-time/on-budget), average project value
- **Targets:** Refer to latest AOP for studio count targets, unit delivery targets, and GTV projections. Strategy: deepening existing markets rather than geographic expansion.
- **Slide context:** Show studio expansion map, project pipeline funnel (leads → design → construction → delivery), unit economics per studio, customer NPS, material cross-sell from platform

#### Credit — 3P (Fintech Orchestration)
- **What:** Tech-led credit orchestration across partner NBFCs and banks, embedded in platform checkout
- **Entity:** Operated under JOPL fintech stack
- **Products:** BNPL (Buy Now Pay Later), channel finance for distributors, factoring
- **Revenue model:** Credit origination fees, commission from lending partners
- **Key metrics:** Credit GMV, disbursals, partner count, penetration (% of GMV on credit), default rates
- **Slide context:** Show credit penetration by BU, partner stack, embedded checkout flow. Source all concentration data from user-provided files.

#### Credit — 1P (JOFL)
- **What:** Captive NBFC for direct lending to platform customers using proprietary data underwriting
- **Entity:** JOFL (JSW One Finance Limited) — wholly-owned subsidiary
- **Products:** Purchase finance, working capital loans
- **Revenue model:** Net interest margin, processing fees
- **Key metrics:** AUM, NPA, disbursals, cost of funds, collection efficiency
- **Phases:** Phase 1 (JSW ecosystem) → Phase 2 (JOPL ecosystem) → Phase 3 (open architecture B2B SME lending)
- **Slide context:** Show AUM growth, NPA trends, portfolio quality, cost of funds optimization

### 11.5 Strategic Narrative — Capital Efficiency Story

JSW One's core investor thesis is capital efficiency, not gross margin:

- **Revenue-to-capital ratio:** Significantly higher than peers (refer to latest corporate presentation for current ratio)
- **Working capital cycle:** Industry-leading low NWC days vs peers
- **Gross margin:** Deliberately lower than peers — trade-off for scale velocity. This is a feature, not a weakness.
- **Hybrid model rationale:** Mix of JODL (1P) for margin generation and 3P marketplace for scale. 100% 1P would require significantly more capital. 100% 3P at low take rates cannot cover platform costs.
- **Key competitors:** OfBusiness, Infra.Market, Zetwerk, Moglix, SG Mart, Shankara Buildpro, Birla Pivot, Tata Nexarc
- **For specific ratios, peer comparisons, and benchmarks — always source from the user-provided data file, latest corporate presentation, or DRHP working papers. Do not hardcode financial metrics in slides.**
